About Me

My name is Kalinda "Kali" Fraser. I aspire to help the world with my work and innovations. For now, though, I'm busy learning as much as I can in my last two quarters in Computer Science at UC Santa Cruz.

Coming from a homeschooling background and a lifetime love of learning, I started school at UCSC with hardware experience from my hobby of amateur radio : I obtained the highest "Extra Class" license at the age of 16, have given presentations at the Pacificon radio convention and the CAKE technical discussion group , have created and facilitated an on-air radio contest with 500+ participants from nearly a dozen countries, was awarded a scholarship for excellence in amateur radio, and have had various mentions in Amateur Radio articles (you may see me listed under "San", a former nickname).

Since starting at UCSC, I have found an affinity for coding, and have gained considerable experience in Java and C programming. I also have some experience with Python, assembly language, and using git and Matlab, as well as basic web design.

As an endlessly curious person, I have developed a wide range of hobbies, not all of which I have the desired time to pursue. As well as amateur radio, I am fascinated with strategy games (and have been an enthusiastic and sometimes competitive go player since I was five), and am a blue belt in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu under Claudio Franca.
